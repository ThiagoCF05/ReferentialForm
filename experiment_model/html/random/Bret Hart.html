Bret Hart

He (born July 2, 1957 in Calgary, Alberta) is a Canadian former professional wrestler, and part of the famous Hart wrestling family. In the course of this Canadian former professional wrestler's career, this Canadian former professional wrestler was known by several monikers: Bret "Hit Man" Hart, (adopted from boxer Thomas Hearns), "The Excellence of Execution" (originally dubbed as such by Gorilla Monsoon) and perhaps the most resounding, "The best there is, the best there was, and the best there ever will be" (derived from the 1984 film The Natural, which starred Robert Redford).

Hart justifies this Canadian former professional wrestler's claim to the latter title by citing three facts: that Hart never seriously injured an opponent through any fault of Hart's own; that, in the course of Hart's career with the World Wrestling Federation, Hart only ever missed two shows, both as a result of traffic and flight difficulties, and that the Canadian former professional wrestler rarely refused to job.
