Frank Whittle

Sir Frank Whittle , OM, KBE FRS (1 June 1907-9 August 1996) was a Royal Air Force officer who invented the jet engine. Due to funding and manufacturing problems and a lack of foresight on the part of the British Government in keeping sensitive patents secret, Hans von Ohain of Germany was able to beat Whittle to production models but Whittle's work and tireless efforts to produce Whittle's designs given official lack of interest are legendary. By the end of the war, this Royal Air Force officer's efforts and Britain's superior high-temperature alloys resulted in engines that led the world in performance for the next decade. Whittle and Ohain met after the war and initially the Royal Air Force officer was angry with him as he felt Ohain had stolen the Royal Air Force officer's ideas. Ohain eventually convinced Whittle that his work was independent and after that point the two became good friends.
