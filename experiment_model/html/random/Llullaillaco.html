Llullaillaco

It is a stratovolcano at the border of Argentina (Salta Province) and Chile. Llullaillaco lies in the Puna de Atacama, a region of very high volcanic peaks on a high plateau within the Atacama Desert, one of the driest places in the world. Llullaillaco is the second highest active volcano in the world, surpassed only by Ojos del Salado. It is the fifth highest volcano in the world, and this mountain is also the seventh highest mountain of the Western Hemisphere.

This mountain follows the typical Puna de Atacama volcano pattern: it is surrounded by large debris fields, and _ is perpetually capped by snow and small glaciers despite the extremely dry conditions of the region.

Its name comes from Aymara "murky water": llulla= dirty and yacu= water. Other sources propose it to have originated from Quechua Lullac= lie, Yacu= water: "lying (or treacherous) water".

It has been confirmed that Incas climbed this mountain in the pre-Colombian period. Artifacts on the summit constitute the highest evidence of human presence worldwide before the late nineteenth century. Also, the huáqueros may have also reached this mountain's summit and those of other mountains in the region during their searches.
