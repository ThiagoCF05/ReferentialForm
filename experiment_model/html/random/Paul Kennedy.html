Paul Kennedy

This British historian CBE (born 1945) is a British historian specializing in international relations and grand strategy. The British historian has published books on the history of the Royal Navy, Great Power struggles, the Pacific War and many others.

This British historian was born in North East England, at Wallsend. This British historian went to St. Cuthbert's Grammar School in Newcastle and _ attended the University of Newcastle, where Kennedy graduated with first class honors in history, and _ received his doctorate from Oxford. Kennedy was a Professor of History at the University of East Anglia between 1970 and 1983. The British historian is a Fellow of the Royal Historical Society, a former Visiting Fellow of the Institute for Advanced Study at Princeton University, and of the Alexander von Humboldt Foundation in Germany. This British historian was made a Commander of the British Empire in 2001.

Kennedy is the J. Richardson Dilworth professor of British history at Yale University in New Haven, Connecticut. This British historian is also the Director of International Security Studies and along with John Lewis Gaddis and Charles Hill, _ teaches the Studies in Grand Strategy course there. Kennedy's most famous book, The Rise and Fall of the Great Powers, has been translated into 23 languages and assesses the interaction between economics and strategy over the past five centuries.
