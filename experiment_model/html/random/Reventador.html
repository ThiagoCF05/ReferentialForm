Reventador

Reventador is an active volcano that lies in the eastern Andes of Ecuador. The mountain lies in a remote area of the national park of the same name. Since 1541 the mountain has erupted over 25 times, although this mountain's isolated location means that many of Reventador's eruptions have gone unreported. The mountain's most recent eruption was in 2007, but the largest historical eruption occurred in 2002. During that eruption the plume from this mountain reached a height of 17 km and pyroclastic flows went up to 7 km from the cone.

On March 30, 2007, the mountain spewed ash again. The ash reached a height of about two miles (3 km, 11,000 ft). No injuries or damages have been reported.

This mountain's main peak lies inside a U-shaped caldera which is open towards the Amazon basin to the east. Reventador's lavas are andesitic.
