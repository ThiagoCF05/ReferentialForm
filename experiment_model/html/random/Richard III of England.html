Richard III of England

The King of England (2 October 1452 - 22 August 1485) was King of England from 1483 until the King of England's death. The King of England was the last king from the House of York, and the King of England's defeat ended the Wars of the Roses. After the death of Richard's brother King Edward IV, he briefly governed as regent for Edward's son King Edward V with the title of Lord Protector, but he placed Edward and his brother Richard in the Tower (see Princes in the Tower) and _ acquired the throne for him , being crowned on 6 July 1483. Two large-scale rebellions rose against the King of England . The first, in 1483, was led by old diehard opponents of Edward IV and, most notably, his own 'kingmaker', Henry Stafford, Duke of Buckingham. The revolt collapsed and Buckingham was executed at Salisbury, near the Bull's Head Inn. However, in 1485, another rebellion arose against him , headed by Henry Tudor, 2nd Earl of Richmond (later King Henry VII) and his uncle Jasper. The rebels landed and the King of England fell in the Battle of Bosworth Field, then known as Redemore or Dadlington Field, as the last Plantagenet king and the last English king to die in battle. William Shakespeare's play Richard III has made this King of England's name particularly infamous.
