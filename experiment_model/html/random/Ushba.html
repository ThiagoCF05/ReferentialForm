Ushba

The mountain ([Georgian: უშბა]) is one of the most notable peaks of the Caucasus Mountains. The mountain is located in the Svaneti region of Georgia, just south of the border with the Kabardino-Balkaria region of Russia. Although the mountain does not rank in the 10 highest peaks of the range, this mountain is known as the "Matterhorn of the Caucasus" for the mountain's picturesque, spire-shaped double summit. Due to this mountain's steep profile and unstable weather, this mountain is considered by many climbers as the most difficult ascent in the Caucasus.

Ushba's south summit is slightly higher than Ushba's north summit, which has an elevation of 4690 m. The north summit was first climbed in 1888 by John Garford Cokklin and Ulrich Almer, while the south summit saw its first ascent in 1903 by a German-Swiss-Austrian expedition led by B. Rickmer-Rickmers.

Its north summit is more accessible than the south summit: the standard route, the Northeast Ridge, ascends from the Russian side of the range to a high plateau and thence to the summit. (Hence a summit ascent on this route technically involves crossing the border.) The route is graded French AD+ or Russian 4a. Routes on the south summit, from the Georgian side, include two routes graded French ED.
