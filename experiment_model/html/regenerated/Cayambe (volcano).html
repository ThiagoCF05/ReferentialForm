Cayambe (volcano)

Cayambe (or Volcán Cayambe) is the name of a volcano located in the Cordillera Oriental, a branch of the Ecuadorian Andes. Cayambe is located in Pichincha province some 70 km (43 mi) northeast of Quito. It is the third highest mountain in Ecuador.

Cayambe , that has a permanent snow cap, is a Holocene compound volcano that has not erupted in historical times. At 4,690 m (15,387 ft) on its south slope is the highest point in the world crossed by the Equator and the only point on the Equator with snow cover (see, for example, Google Earth images). Cayambe is located within the Cayambe-Coca Ecological Reserve.

The town of Cayambe takes its name from Cayambe .

Cayambe was first scaled by British climber Edward Whymper and his two Italian guides and companions Juan Antonio and Luis Carrel in 1880, and _ is a favourite of mountain climbers today.
