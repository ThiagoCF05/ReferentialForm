Eritrea

The modern nation-state of Eritrea (Ge'ez ኤርትራ ʾĒrtrā) is a country in northern East Africa that neighbours Ethiopia. The name is derived from the Latin word for Red Sea, Mare Erythraeum, itself derived from the Greek word meaning "red" (ερυθρός , erythros). Eritrea is bordered by Sudan in the west, Ethiopia in the south, and Djibouti in the southeast. The east and northeast of it have an extensive coastline on the Red Sea, directly across from Saudi Arabia and Yemen. The Dahlak Archipelago and several of the Hanish Islands are part of Eritrea.

Eritrea was consolidated into a colony by the Italian government on January 1, 1890. Eritrea gained its independence from Ethiopia following a thirty year war which lasted from 1961 to 1991. It is officially a parliamentary democracy consisting of six regions, but it currently functions as a single-party state. It is a multilingual and multicultural country with two dominant religions and nine nationalities, each speaking a different language. It has no official language, but it has three working languages: Tigrinya, Arabic, and English. A fourth language, Italian, is sometimes used commercially.
