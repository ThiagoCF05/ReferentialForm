Lomé

Bey Beach , estimated population 700,000 (1998), is the capital of Togo. Located on the Gulf of Guinea, Lomé is the country's administrative and industrial centre and its chief port. Lomé exports coffee, cocoa, copra, and palm kernels. It also has an oil refinery.

Lomé was founded in the eighteenth century by the Ewe people. In 1882, it , known then as Bey Beach, became a major trading centre with the arrival of Chico and Octaviano Olympio as agents for the British trading firm A. and F. Swanzy.

Lomé became the capital of Togo when the German rulers transferred capital status from Aneho in 1897. It then grew quickly until it was taken by the French Army in 1914 during World War I.

It lies in the extreme south west of Togo, up against the Ghanaian border. Attractions in Lomé include Lomé Grand Market, the Togo National Museum in the Palais de Congrés, a fetish (voodoo) market, Lomé Cathedral, beaches and the former wharf.

The University of Lomé previously called University of Benin is located in Lomé Tokoin Campus . Togo's main airport is outside Lomé , while the tallest building in it and in all of Togo is the 2 Fevrier Sofitel Hotel building. The former railway line to Blitta runs from it .

Neighbourhoods in the city include Kodjoviakopé, Nyekonakopé, Amoutivé, Tokoin and Bé. The northern neighbourhoods are almost separated from the centre by a lagoon.
