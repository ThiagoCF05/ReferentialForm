Mount Apo

Mount Apo is a large stratovolcano on the island of Mindanao in the Philippines. Apo is the highest mountain in the country and _ overlooks Davao City, a few kilometres to the northeast. Its name means "master" or "grandfather". It is flat topped, with three peaks, and _ is capped by a 500 m wide volcanic crater containing a small crater lake. It is a source of geothermal energy, but the date of its most recent eruption is unknown, and none are verified in historic times.

Apo is one of the most popular climbing destinations in the Philippines, and the summit is easy to reach. Apo was first climbed on October 10 1880 by a party led by Don Joaquin Rajal.
