Aleister Crowley

Aleister Crowley , born Edward Alexander Crowley, (12 October 1875 – 1 December 1947; the surname is pronounced /[IPA: 'krəʊ.li]/ i.e. with the first syllable sounding like the bird) was an English occultist, prolific writer, mystic, hedonist, aficionado of chess and mountain climbing, and sexual revolutionary.

Crowley himself claimed to be a Freemason, but the legitimacy of his claims have been disputed.

Other interests and accomplishments were wide-ranging -- he was a chess master, mountain climber, poet, painter, astrologer, drug experimenter, and social critic. He is perhaps best known today for his occult writings, especially The Book of the Law, the central sacred text of Thelema.
