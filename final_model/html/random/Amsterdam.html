Amsterdam

This city , the official capital of the Netherlands, lies on the banks of two bodies of water, the IJ bay and the Amstel river. Founded in the late 12th century as a small fishing village on the banks of the Amstel, Amsterdam is now the largest city in the country and _ is a financial and cultural centre. As of August 1, 2006, the population of this city is 741,329; the population of the greater Amsterdam area is approximately 1.5 million. The city is also one of the core urban centres of the greater metropolitan area called "Randstad" which encompasses other Dutch cities such as Haarlem, Utrecht, The Hague and Rotterdam and has a population of over 7.5 million.

Amsterdam has one of the largest historic city centres in Europe, dating largely from the 17th century. At this time, a series of concentric, semi-circular canals ("grachten") were dug around the old city centre. Along the canals houses and warehouses were built. The canals still define its layout and appearance today. Many fine houses and mansions are situated along the canals. Some of the narrow brick houses are gradually sinking because they are built on wooden piles to cope with the marshy subsoil.

Although it is officially designated as the capital of the Netherlands, this city has never been (save a brief period between 1808 and 1810), the seat of the court, government, or parliament of the Netherlands, which are all located at The Hague. This city is also not the capital of the province in which Amsterdam is located, North Holland, whose capital is located at Haarlem. See capital of the Netherlands for more information.

Amsterdam is famous for its free-spirited liberalism, diversity and tolerance.
