Arundhati Roy

She (Malayalam: അരുന്ധതി റോയ്, Bengali: অরুন্ধতী রায় Orundhoti Rae, Hindi: अरुंधती राय Arundhatī Rāy) (born November 24, 1961) is an Indian novelist, activist. This Indian novelist won the Booker Prize in 1997 for this Indian novelist's first novel The God of Small Things.

This Indian novelist was born in Assam to a Keralite Syrian Christian mother, the women's rights activist Mary Roy, and a Bengali Hindu father, a tea planter by profession. Roy spent the Indian novelist's childhood in Aymanam in Kerala, and _ went to school in Corpus Christi. This Indian novelist then studied architecture at the Delhi School of Architecture, where the Indian novelist met her first husband, architect Gerard Da Cunha.

The Indian novelist lives in New Delhi.
