Ellingwood Point

Ellingwood Point , or Ellingwood Peak, is a fourteener in the U.S. state of Colorado. Ellingwood Point is located at the southern end of the Sangre de Cristo Range, approximately 1/2 mile (1 km) northwest of Blanca Peak. The mountain lies on the border between Alamosa County and Huerfano County. The mountain is named for Albert Russell Ellingwood, an early pioneer of climbing in the Western United States and in Colorado in particular.

The mountain only barely qualifies as an independent peak under the 300 foot topographic prominence cutoff which is standard in Colorado. Ellingwood Point is also quite close to the mountain's parent peak, Blanca Peak. Hence its inclusion in fourteener lists has been somewhat controversial. However most authorities do consider Ellingwood Point a true fourteener. Because of Ellingwood Point's proximity to Blanca Peak, the mountain is often climbed together with that peak in a single day.
