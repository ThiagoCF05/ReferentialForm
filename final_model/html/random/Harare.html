Harare

This city (pronounced [IPA: /'hʌ ɹɑ' ɹi/], formerly Salisbury) is the capital city of Zimbabwe. Harare has an estimated population 1,600,000, with 2,800,000 in the city's metropolitan area (2006). Harare is Zimbabwe's largest city and its administrative, commercial, and communications centre. The city is a trade centre for tobacco, maize, cotton, and citrus fruits. Manufactures include textiles, steel, and chemicals. Gold is mined in the area.. this city is at an elevation of 4,865 feet (1,483 metres) and _ has a temperate climate.

Despite the renaming of it , some suburbs have retained their European names, such as Warren Park 'D', Borrowdale, Mount Pleasant, Tynwald and Avondale.

This city is famous for Harare's beautiful jacaranda-lined streets, especially in the "Avenues" suburbs to the immediate north of the city centre.

The city is the site of the University of Zimbabwe, the largest and most complete institution of higher learning in Zimbabwe.
