Moscow

It ([Russian: Москва́], Moskva, IPA: [mʌsk'va]) is the capital of Russia and the country's principal political, economic, financial, educational, and transportation center, located on the Moskva River in the Central Federal District, located in the European part of Russia. Moscow's population of 10.4 million permanent inhabitants within the city boundaries constitutes about 7% of the total Russian population. Likewise, Moscow is the most populous city in Europe. Historically, the city's position was central in the Russian homeland. The city was the capital of the former Soviet Union and Muscovite Russia, the pre-Imperial Russian state. The city is the site of the famous Kremlin, which now serves as the ceremonial residence of the President.

This city's architecture and performing arts culture are world-renowned. Moscow is also well known as the site of Saint Basil's Cathedral, with its elegant onion domes, as well as the Cathedral of Christ the Savior. The Patriarch of Moscow, whose residence is the Danilov Monastery, serves as the head of the Russian Orthodox Church. Moscow also remains a major economic center and _ is home to a large number of billionaires; it is perennially considered one of the most expensive cities for expatriate employees in the world. Moscow is home to many scientific and educational institutions, as well as numerous sport facilities. It possesses a complex transport system that includes the world's busiest metro system, which is famous for its architecture. The city also became the host of the 1980 Summer Olympics.
