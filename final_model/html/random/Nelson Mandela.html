Nelson Mandela

Nelson Rolihlahla Mandela (IPA ) (born July 18, 1918) was the first President of South Africa to be elected in fully-representative democratic elections. Before Mandela's presidency he was a prominent anti-apartheid activist and leader of the African National Congress. Mandela was tried and imprisoned for this first President's involvement in underground armed resistance activities. The armed struggle was a last resort; Mandela had remained steadfastly committed to non-violence. Through this first President's 27-year imprisonment, much of it spent in a cell on Robben Island, this first President became the most widely known figure in the struggle against South African apartheid. Although the apartheid regime and nations sympathetic to it considered Mandela and the ANC to be communists and terrorists, the armed struggle was an integral part of the overall campaign against apartheid. The switch in policy to that of reconciliation, which this first President pursued upon the first President's release in 1990, facilitated a peaceful transition to fully-representative democracy in South Africa.

Having received over a hundred awards over four decades, he is currently a celebrated elder statesman who continues to voice this first President's opinion on topical issues. In South Africa he is often known as Madiba, an honorary title adopted by elders of this first President's clan. The title has come to be synonymous with the first President . Many South Africans also refer to him reverently as 'mkhulu' (grandfather).
