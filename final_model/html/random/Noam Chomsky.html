Noam Chomsky

Avram Noam Chomsky , Ph.D (born December 7, 1928) is the Institute Professor Emeritus of linguistics at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. The Institute Professor Emeritus of linguistics at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology is credited with the creation of the theory of generative grammar, considered to be one of the most significant contributions to the field of theoretical linguistics made in the 20th century. He also helped spark the cognitive revolution in psychology through Chomsky's review of B.F. Skinner's Verbal Behavior, in which this Institute Professor Emeritus of linguistics at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology challenged the behaviorist approach to the study of mind and language dominant in the 1950s. His naturalistic approach to the study of language has also affected the philosophy of language and mind (see Harman, Fodor). The Institute Professor Emeritus of linguistics at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology is also credited with the establishment of the Chomsky–Schützenberger hierarchy, a classification of formal languages in terms of their generative power.

Beginning with Chomsky's critique of the Vietnam War in the 1960s, Chomsky has become more widely known - especially internationally - for the Institute Professor Emeritus of linguistics at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology's media criticism and radical politics than for his linguistic theories. He is generally considered to be a key intellectual figure within the left wing of United States politics. According to the Arts and Humanities Citation Index, between 1980 and 1992 the Institute Professor Emeritus of linguistics at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology was cited as a source more often than any other living scholar, and the eighth most cited scholar overall. Chomsky is widely known for this Institute Professor Emeritus of linguistics at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology's political activism, and for the Institute Professor Emeritus of linguistics at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology's criticism of the foreign policy of the United States and other governments. The Institute Professor Emeritus of linguistics at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology describes Chomsky as a libertarian socialist and a sympathizer of anarcho-syndicalism ( the Institute Professor Emeritus of linguistics at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology is a member of the IWW).
