Pigeon Peak

This mountain is a high peak of the Needle Mountains, a subrange of the San Juan Mountains in the southwestern part of the US State of Colorado. The mountain rises dramatically on the east side of the Animas River, 1.5 mi (2.4 km) west of the fourteener Mount Eolus. The mountain is located in the Weminuche Wilderness, part of the San Juan National Forest.

This mountain is notable both for Pigeon Peak's absolute height and for its local relief in particular. The mountain is the 57th highest independent peak in Colorado, narrowly missing the well-known list of fourteeners. In terms of local relief, the mountain is one of the most impressive peaks in Colorado. Pigeon Peak's most dramatic rise is over the Animas River to the west, over which the mountain rises 5,750 ft (1,750 m) in under 2.5 mi (4 km). Also, this mountain's east face is a 1,000 ft (305 m) cliff.
