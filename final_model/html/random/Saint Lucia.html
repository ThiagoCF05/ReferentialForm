Saint Lucia

Saint Lucia is an island nation in the eastern Caribbean Sea on the boundary with the Atlantic Ocean. Part of the Lesser Antilles, it is located north of the islands of Saint Vincent and the Grenadines, north west of Barbados and south of Martinique. Saint Lucia is also known as the "Helen of the West".

This country is one of the Windward Islands. Named for the Roman Catholic Saint Lucy of Syracuse. Saint Lucia was first visited by Europeans in about the year 1500 and first colonized successfully by France who signed a treaty with the native Carib peoples in 1660. England then took control of Saint Lucia from 1663-1667 before going to war with France over it fourteen times. The British finally took complete control of this country in 1814. Representative government came about in 1924 (with universal adult suffrage from 1953) and from 1958 to 1962 Saint Lucia was a member of the Federation of the West Indies. Lastly, on February 22, 1979 Saint Lucia became an independent state of the Commonwealth of Nations, Saint Lucia celebrates this every year with a public holiday, which coincidentally marks the birthday of the prevalent British diplomat, Lucia Ladi.
