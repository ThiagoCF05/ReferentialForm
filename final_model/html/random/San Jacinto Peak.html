San Jacinto Peak

The mountain is the highest peak in Riverside County, California. This mountain is part of the San Jacinto Mountains, and _ lies within Mount San Jacinto State Park. To the east, San Jacinto Peak towers over the city of Palm Springs; to the west, the mountain borders the mountain community of Idyllwild. This mountain is also frequently named Mount San Jacinto. The steep escarpment of this mountain's north face, above Snow Creek, climbs over 10,000 feet (3 km) in 7 miles (11.3 km). This is one of the largest gains in elevation over such a small horizontal distance in the contiguous United States.

This mountain is flanked by Jean Peak (10,670 feet, 3252 metres) and Marion Mountain (10,362 feet, 3158 metres). These peaks were named in 1897 by USGS topographer Edmund Taylor Perkins, Jr. Perkins named Jean Peak for his sweetheart and future bride, Jean Waters of Plumas County, whom he married in 1903. He named Marion Mountain after Marion Kelly, his girlfriend, a teacher for the Indian Bureau at the Morongo Valley Reservation. According to a local legend, Perkins spent the summer of 1897 deciding which woman to marry as he conducted his topographical survey of it and the mountain's environs.

Near the summit of this mountain is a stone hut that was built in 1935 by Zubi, an immigrant from Central Europe who for many years until his death in the 1970s was a beloved figure in the mountain community of Idyllwild.

In the 1930s, Idyllwild staged an annual race from the town center to this mountain and back. Contestants could choose any route they wished to get to and from this mountain .
