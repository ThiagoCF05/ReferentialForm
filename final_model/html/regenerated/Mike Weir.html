Mike Weir

Michael Richard Weir O.Ont. (born May 12, 1970) is a professional golfer on the PGA Tour.

Mike was born in Sarnia, Ontario, Canada. He is a graduate of Brigham Young University (majoring in Recreation Management), and he turned pro in 1992. Him's first PGA Tour win came at the 1999 Air Canada Championship in Surrey, British Columbia. The victory made Mike the first Canadian to win a PGA Tour event in Canada in 45 years.

On April 13, 2003, Mike won the prestigious Masters Tournament at Augusta, Georgia, one of the four Major tournaments in men's golf. Mike is the first Canadian ever to win a professional major championship (Sandy Somerville won the U.S. Amateur when it was considered a major tournament). When he won The Masters, he became only the second left-handed golfer to win any of the four majors, the other being Bob Charles, who won the British Open 40 years earlier. He is a right-hander who plays golf left-handed; a trait he shares with fellow PGA Tour pro Phil Mickelson.

In June 2003, Mike tied for third at the U.S. Open, the second of the majors in the annual schedule. For Mike's outstanding play in 2003, he won the Lou Marsh Award for outstanding Canadian athlete of the year and for a time in 2003 and 2004 he was in the top ten in PGA Tour player rankings and in the top 3 in the Official World Golf Rankings.

In February 2004, Mike joined the ranks of a select few players including Ben Hogan to win back to back championships at the Nissan Open, becoming the sixth player in Nissan Open history to notch back-to-back wins and the first since Corey Pavin (1994, 1995). is the 20th player to post multiple wins at the Nissan Open.

He currently lives in Draper, Utah, with him's wife Bricia and two daughters. Him's wife is a member of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, though Mike himself is not. Him's home course is the Taboo Golf Club in Gravenhurst, Ontario.
